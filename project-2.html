Software private offers

Overview

Software private offers (code-named Skybridge) was a cross-business initiative between IBM Cloud and IBM Software to define the end-to-end quote-to-cash experience for IBM SaaS subscriptions on IBM Cloud. The work aimed to preserve software profitability, support seller compensation, and achieve parity with how IBM SaaS is sold on other hyperscalers, while creating a clear model for how subscriptions are ordered, entitled, provisioned, and tracked. My role focused on bringing clarity to this highly complex problem space through workflow mapping, cross-team alignment, and scenario-based UX flows that became reference artifacts for product, engineering, and leadership.

As a Senior UX Designer, I helped shape the end-to-end experience for private offers spanning IBM Cloud and IBM Software. I mapped existing workflows, identified gaps and opportunities, facilitated working sessions, and designed system-level flows that aligned multiple teams on a single private-offer model. I partnered closely with PMs, architects, and engineers to codify requirements, expose dependencies, and create artifacts that could be used to guide both product strategy and implementation.

The problem

IBM Software’s strategy was to sell SaaS subscription entitlements for their products via IBM Cloud—not just as generic cloud spend, but as product-specific commitments that preserve margins and align with SaaS seller compensation. However, the only ordering paths available on IBM Cloud were pay-as-you-go plans, broad platform subscriptions/credits, or manual entitlements. None of these paths met the needs of the Software organization or motivated their sellers to sell on IBM's cloud over other hyperscalers.

At the same time, IBM needed to offer a quote-to-cash experience for IBM SaaS on IBM Cloud that was at least on par with how those same products are sold on other hyperscalers. Anything less would undermine both the platform and the SaaS portfolio. The lack of a dedicated SaaS subscription model on IBM Cloud also made it difficult to ensure product-level revenue attribution, customer commitment burndown, and seller compensation.

Key challenges included:

  • No dedicated SaaS subscription model on IBM Cloud that matched Software’s business needs
  • Existing paths (paygo, generic cloud subscriptions, manual entitlements) could not preserve margins or support SaaS seller compensation
  • Lack of parity with IBM SaaS ordering flows on other hyperscalers
  • No single, unified model for how SaaS subscriptions should burn down against IBM Cloud account commitments
  • Heavy reliance on manual processes that did not scale and made cancellations, renewals, and corrections difficult

Business context and goals

From a business perspective, Skybridge needed to:

  • Preserve software profitability by supporting discrete SaaS commitments instead of relying solely on paygo models
  • Incentivize larger customer commits through subscription and overage structures aligned with Software pricing
  • Ensure product-level revenue attribution for SaaS subscriptions, not just generic platform spend
  • Achieve parity with how IBM SaaS can be ordered on other hyperscalers
  • Align the ordering model with SaaS seller compensation so that catalog orders would not harm seller incentives

Process

As requirements were nailed down, the sketching began to increase in fidelity. Views like this helped our stakeholders see how the whole system impact's the user's experience.

With so many systems and organizations involved (Cloud, Software, sellers, partners, billing, usage, entitlement) every UX decision had ripple effects across teams. Ensuring alignment required a level of collaboration that was difficult to achieve under normal circumstances.

  • Regular working sessions
    One of the biggest wins of the project was securing daily standing calls with busy senior stakeholders. These were highly productive sessions where we clarified requirements, surfaced hidden dependencies, and made rapid decisions that kept the work on track.
  • Parity with other hyperscalers
    Sellers shared a surprising insight that selling IBM Software was often easier on other clouds than on IBM’s own. We conducted a comparative analysis of the private offer flows from leading hyperscalers and used those insights to set the baseline for our experience.

    • Regular co-creation sessions with architects and PMs helped us deepen our understanding of how pricing, contracts, and provisioning systems needed to work together.

  • Understanding technical limitations
    This project depended on accurately understanding how information moved through the system. Identifying what data the APIs exposed at each stage from quote to offer to subscription to pricing plan was essential for determining what the UI could display and how the experience should be structured.

    • Understanding what information we could access through the API and display in the UI.

  • Scenario-based UX flows
    Created flows for key scenarios such as creating a private offer, revising terms, bundling multiple products, and handling renewals.

    • Scenario flows for seller and partner journeys, used in working sessions to align on states, responsibilities, and edge cases.

    Outcomes

    • Defined a target quote-to-cash model for IBM SaaS subscriptions on IBM Cloud.

      The unified workflows made it clear where existing Cloud systems could support Software’s subscription and compensation model. Skybridge produced a shared, end-to-end model that both organizations could use from private offer configuration to provisioning.
