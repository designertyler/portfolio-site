Cloud migration flows

A comprehensive redesign of a multi-step cloud migration workflow to reduce friction, clarify key decision points, and support both technical and business users through a complex transition process.

Project details

Role: Lead UX Designer

Timeframe: 12 weeks

Team: 1 PM, 2 engineers, 1 content designer, 1 researcher

Tools: Figma, Mural, Jira, Confluence

The problem

The existing cloud migration experience lacked a clear structure and relied heavily on tribal knowledge. Users struggled to understand prerequisites, identify required steps, and determine who needed to complete each part of the process. This resulted in:

  • High drop-off early in the workflow
  • Frequent support escalations
  • Unclear handoff points between engineering and operations teams
  • Inconsistent success criteria and migration readiness

Context

This workflow supports migrations from legacy on-prem environments into a managed cloud offering. Users vary widely—from hands-on engineers to project managers responsible for coordinating multi-team efforts. Research showed that although the needs differ, all users required better visibility into requirements, progress, and dependencies.

Goals

  • Create a predictable, structured workflow that reduces confusion
  • Support different users with varying levels of technical expertise
  • Surface prerequisites and dependencies clearly and early
  • Reduce support tickets through a more self-guided experience

Constraints

  • Needed to integrate with existing backend APIs and data models
  • Could not change the order of some technical steps
  • Limited engineering capacity during the first two sprints
  • Required alignment across three different product teams

Process

The design process followed an iterative, collaborative structure:

  1. Co-creation sessions with PM, engineering, and SMEs
  2. Mapping current state to identify friction points and dependencies
  3. Workshopping new flows to consolidate fragmented steps
  4. Wireframes and prototypes tested with internal users
  5. Iterating based on feedback to improve clarity and reduce cognitive load

Outcomes

  • Reduced workflow length from 14 steps to 8 core steps
  • Clearer branching logic for technical vs. business paths
  • Improved visibility into migration status and readiness
  • Support tickets related to migration setup dropped an estimated 28%

Next steps

Future improvements include deeper integration with automated validation tools, expanded in-context guidance, and a dashboard for tracking multiple migrations across teams.
